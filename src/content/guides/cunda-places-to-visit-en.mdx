Cunda Island — officially Alibey Island — is one of the Aegean's most distinctive corners. With its historic stone houses, centuries-old olive trees, and turquoise coves, it's the jewel of the Ayvalık coast. Here's your guide to the island's unmissable landmarks, tracing the footsteps of its [remarkable history](/en/guide/cunda-history).

## Taksiyarhis Memorial Museum (Church of Ayios Yiorgis)

Cunda's most iconic structure, the Taksiyarhis Church was built in the 19th century as a grand Greek Orthodox church. Today it serves as a memorial museum, its interior frescoes and imposing bell tower revealing the island's deep multicultural heritage. It is the most tangible and best-preserved example of Greek legacy on Cunda.

## The Waterfront Promenade

Starting from the harbor and stretching to the island's western tip, the coastal walkway is ideal for morning strolls and sunset appreciation alike. Along the route you'll pass historic fishing shelters, restored stone houses, and panoramic viewpoints overlooking the Aegean. [Our hotel, Milos Cunda](/en/rooms), sits right at the heart of this promenade.

## Sevim & Necdet Kent Library

Housed in a beautifully restored stone building, this library is notable both for its architecture and its extensive regional archive. Its quiet courtyard makes a perfect stop for reading and coffee between explorations.

## Rahmi M. Koç Museum Cunda

Set inside a former olive oil factory, this industrial museum tells the story of the region's olive oil production through interactive exhibits. A family-friendly experience, it offers a different lens on [Cunda's gastronomic heritage](/en/guide/aegean-gastronomy).

## Pateriça Forest & Şeytan Sofrası (Devil's Table)

The Pateriça Forest on the island's northern end offers nature walks through pine trees and maquis shrubland. At the forest's edge, the Şeytan Sofrası rock formation rewards hikers with a sweeping panoramic view across the Ayvalık archipelago. After the walk, cool off at one of [Cunda's beaches](/en/guide/cunda-beaches).

## Street Art & Galleries

Cunda's narrow backstreets reveal wall murals, sculptures, and independent art galleries at every turn. Summer brings open-air exhibitions that reflect the island's creative energy. Small workshops along the lanes showcase the work of local artists — ceramics, textiles, and paintings inspired by the Aegean light.
